Frustrated Total Internal Reflection (FTIR) — when a gap (medium 2, low n) separates two high-n prisms, the evanescent field from TIR penetrates the gap and couples into prism 2 if d ≲ δ (decay length). Transmission T recovers exponentially as d decreases.
n₁ prism (high index) 1.50
n₂ gap medium 1.00
θᵢ incidence (°) 50°
Gap d (× λ) 0.50 λ
TIR condition: n₁ sinθᵢ > n₂
Decay length: δ = λ / (2π√(n₁²sin²θᵢ − n₂²))
T ≈ exp(−2d/δ) (p-pol, approximate)
FTIR: significant T when d ≲ δ
